## What we build

**Plinqx** is an **on-platform integration studio** for Salesforce. It lets you design, run, and govern API-driven integrations **inside your Salesforce org** — using familiar Salesforce building blocks like **Flow**, native security (profiles, permission sets), and first-class observability — without standing up external integration servers or heavyweight middleware for the core experience.

We help teams connect Salesforce to both **everyday business tools** and **enterprise platforms**: accounting and finance (e.g. **Xero**, **MYOB**, **QuickBooks**), ERP and operations (e.g. **SAP**, **Oracle**, **NetSuite**, **Workday**), data and cloud ecosystems (e.g. **Snowflake**, **AWS**, **Azure**), telco and custom internal APIs — and more. Our [AppExchange listing](https://appexchange.salesforce.com/appxListingDetail?listingId=a3d3bf3b-c23b-4224-8d93-c77709154839) describes the breadth of systems teams connect with Plinqx.

If your roadmap sounds like *“we need Salesforce to talk to the rest of the business, securely, with clear ownership and auditability”* — that is the problem Plinqx is designed for.

## Why teams choose native integration

| Principle | What it means for you |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Salesforce-native by design** | Runs on the Salesforce Platform — leverage the same trust, identity, and governance model you already operate. |
| **No-code / low-code with Flow** | Admins and analysts can deliver integrations with the tools they already use; reduce dependency on bespoke code for standard patterns. |
| **Unified experience** | One consistent way to connect lightweight SaaS alongside large-scale or custom systems — SMB to enterprise on one model. |
| **Less infrastructure drag** | Inbound and outbound integrations without the classic “rent another box in the middle” operational tax. |
| **Visibility where work happens** | Transactions can be logged and monitored in Salesforce — faster troubleshooting and clearer operational ownership. |

We support proven integration styles — including **point-to-point**, **hub-and-spoke**, **publish/subscribe**, and **file-based** flows — so patterns can evolve as your architecture matures.
